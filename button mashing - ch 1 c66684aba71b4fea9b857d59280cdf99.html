Minho drove a lot of people insane, but with Felix at least, it was never intentional.

"So I press this, and then it'll let me use that skill?"

An animalistic whine sounded from the seat next to him as Felix dragged his fingers down his face. "Hyung, no. That's jump." On-screen, Minho's newly created avatar hopped up and down in place.

"Ah, Lix, don't worry so much." Minho waved a hand dismissively, hurriedly placing it back on the keyboard when his character almost dashed off the top of a cliff. "I'll figure out the controls on my own, okay? Trial and error, or whatever. And then we can play together whenever you want."

Felix squished his cheek into Minho's shoulder, rubbing his face against his hoodie until his hair became a mess again. "Okaaay."

"Go do your homework like a good little student, okay? I know you hate watching me play."

"Only because you won't let me help," Felix complained. "And you're really bad, too. And classes literally just started, so I don't even have homework."

Minho poked the freshman in the forehead, making him lose his balance. "Go. I promise I'll be decent enough to do that birthday quest with you, alright? And what's the point of you grinding my character if I can't familiarize myself with the controls, silly."

Felix nodded glumly, still sulking. "Fine then. I'll go to the canteen and get some snacks. Want anything?"

Minho hummed in thought. "Fried chicken? My wallet's in the top drawer. No, wait, I can't eat that and play at the same time. Just get me a drink."

As Felix let himself out of their shared room, Minho turned back to his screen, frowning a little. Truth be told, he hadn't been paying attention to Felix's introductory spiel at all, and he may or may not have accidentally skipped through the tutorial. So now, with his little character standing in the middle of an open field, with no idea of which direction to move in, Minho was in a bit of a pickle.

It's not like Minho had never played a game before in his life. He'd picked up Pokemon once or twice as a kid, watched occasionally as his classmates would crowd around a computer as someone played League, but he never quite understood the appeal. Real life was hectic enough, thank you very much, and Minho didn't have time to micromanage a half-life in a false reality.

But Felix had batted his lashes, making his cherished request in such a pitiful kitten-like voice, that Minho had no choice but to acquiesce. The happy little cheer his underclassman let out immediately after had let him know he'd been scammed once again, but, well. It's not like it'd be a bad thing for Minho to learn what the kids were up to these days.

The problem was, technology had progressed quite a bit farther since the last time he paid attention to digital entertainment, and this new style of gameplay was a tad bit overwhelming to the inexperienced Minho.

"Whatever, it'll be fine," he muttered to himself, mousing around the screen and tapping random keys to figure out which button did what. Through trial and error and no insubstantial HP loss, Minho managed to uncover his inventory, his skills, his map, and a handful of menus, enough to have him feeling comfortable enough to move around.

This MMORPG (and Minho had already forgotten the name, if he was being honest) seemed to be a generic fantasy world, but to someone who had never played this genre of game before, it was quite exciting.

The character creation process alone was very interesting to Minho, who had had a bit too much fun customizing his character's physical features.

"It's for virtual reality," Felix had explained as he took careful pictures of Minho's face from multiple angles. "Your game character is unique to you, and it's locked to your real face and identity in case they upgrade to VR. A lot of newer games have been messing with the technology lately, and this one's kinda lagging behind in that regard, but there's been rumors that they might update it soon."

"So you're telling me to play an outdated junk game," Minho had teased him, fascinated by the way his face was fitted to the in-game avatar's. It truly did look quite like him, if he had bright white hair and dressed like a medieval peasant.

"Noooo," Felix protested. "It's really fun, I swear, I promise you'll like it."

"You wouldn't let me add the cat ears on him."

"Hyung, demis have crappy stats. I'll buy you a cat ears headband for your character later, okay? You can lock them to your character so they won’t drop even when you die," Felix wheedled.

Beyond his character, Minho had to admit the graphics of the game were stunning, if still a bit unrealistic, to the point of nearly overheating his computer. For now, he played on Felix's fancy gaming laptop, since the younger was apparently looking for a new one soon.

Minho angles his character to look down, admiring the way the grass actually remained crushed after he stomped all over it. This was quite fun in and of itself, but the squeal of a wild pig charging in his direction told him he'd be quickly learning how to fight.

"Bro, where are you?" Changbin barked out, voice so loud it sounded staticky even through Jisung's new headphones.

"I'm on my way, like I said the last 5 times you asked," Jisung complained. "S'not my fault people spammed me as soon as I logged on. I even misclicked on where to teleport."

"Not very humble of you to be complaining about how famous you are," Chan teased from the same voice channel.

Jisung smirked, even though the others couldn't see it. "Well, we can't all be the best player in the game."

"Only in Korea," Changbin quipped grumpily, although Jisung knew he wasn't being mean. "Pretty sure you'd get your ass beat by the Americans."

Jisung stuck his tongue out.

"And stop sticking your tongue out, you're already ugly."

Jisung rolled his eyes as he swiftly clicked towards the correct portal. "Are you two still at the office, hyung?"

"Yeah," came Chan's reply. "There was no way we'd get home in time before the dungeon closed."

Jisung hummed in understanding. Maybe if he took a shortcut through the Adventurer's Prairie, he'd shave a few seconds off his journey. Usually he avoided that place like a plague, but scrolling through the world chat where his previous location had been leaked, it seemed like everyone's attention had been successfully diverted.

"Whew," Jisung muttered to himself, dashing across the open plains. It'd been a long time since he'd come back here. Even now, with the game's newbie influx in decline, there still seemed to be some people dutifully whacking at the low-level Feral Hogs.

Cute, he thought in passing at one rather attractive guy, before sliding into the portal just as it was about to close.

As much as he enjoyed teaming up with his best friends, there was still something comforting about the headspace Jisung got into when he played alone, when he didn't have to waste energy thinking about how to communicate his plays to other people, the particular rhythm between his movements and the sounds of his mouse clicking.

This time, Jisung didn't worry too much about the completion time of his run. Since he was getting through it alone, of course his time would be far from the top of the leaderboard; he mainly wanted to play something familiar.

Without the need to concentrate too much on his every move, Jisung's thoughts wandered. This was his favorite part of gaming, when his hands could fidget enough on autopilot to leave his brain empty in a focused way without truly zoning out, and despite how everyone else perceived him, this was when he felt like he was the most productive.

In his head, Jisung mentally shuffled through some ideas for the designs Chan and Changbin would be expecting, even as his hands moved agilely across the keyboard, scores of mobs dying under his accurate movements. The older two had dropped out partway through university to run a wildly successful merchandise line, with the goal to eventually expand into a full trademarked brand one day. They’d already gone viral a few times on social media, but they were still firmly in the ‘cult following’ tier and hadn’t truly broken through to mainstream popularity yet. That might take years, if not decades.

While Chan was responsible for most of their marketed characters, quirky cartoon doodles brought to life in the form of plushies and plastered across other products, Jisung had been the one responsible for most of the product designs. It'd been his idea for a dino-shaped coffee maker that had earned them their first million from a single product, and after that, the ideas had never stopped coming.

Jisung huffed. His hyungs wouldn't let him quit school to work full-time for them though, stating their concerns that it'd do him more good to be around people other than just them two, along with the benefits a good education generally brought. Joke's on them, though, when Jisung tended to stay aloof even while gaming. Despite being a notorious ranker, his friend list wasn't even halfway full: Jisung’s usual response when other top players asked to stay in touch was that they could simply friend Chan or Changbin if they wanted to get in contact with him. The three of them were inseparable, and both his hyungs were far better at remembering to navigate social niceties than him.

Sighing with relief at the completion notification, Jisung settled back into his chair as he began to make his way back across the continent. Figuring no one saw him besides that one guy, he decided to cut through Adventurer's Prairie again.

To his surprise, the same player was still there, although now he was fighting barehanded against the mobs. Jisung glanced over at his clock. It had already been more than an hour and a half since he had first saw the guy: he must have broken his beginner's sword already.

Jisung couldn't help smiling a bit as he clicked on the other player's status. He wasn't sure if the Level 7 "soondoongdo" was hardworking or simply clueless, but he thought he'd offer a bit of encouragement nonetheless, especially since he was rich enough to be generous.
